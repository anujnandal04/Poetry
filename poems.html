A Love Letter Across Timelines

January 2025 Longing & Alternate Realities

On this birthday of mine… 22.
No party, no noise.
Just me.
And this room.
And somehow, you.

I don't scroll through chats anymore,
but I still remember the exact way your name lit up my screen.
I still hear your laugh in the silence between my thoughts.

I imagine us lying under a sky that doesn't end — a version of us with no goodbyes,
no pauses mid-sentence,
just your voice —
"I'll carry you home…"
playing softly like a lullaby for my heart.

Maybe somewhere,
you're still talking to me at 12 a.m.,
telling me it's going to be okay,
while I trace constellations on the ceiling, pretending they're your freckles.

Maybe I'm waiting outside your office with sunflowers,
or tying your shoelaces before you walk into that favourite cafe of yours.
Maybe I'm not just in love with you —
I get to love you, there.

Maybe in another universe,
I built a swing for you on our terrace,
and you're laughing
as I push you higher than your fears.
You wear my hoodie.
I write poems for your cheeks.
You call me at 3 a.m.
just to ask if stars ever get tired of burning.

And maybe, just maybe,
in that version — you know.
You know how deeply you're adored.
Not just by a boy, but by every word he's ever written.

So tonight, as I step into 22 —
I don't wish for anything.
I already had something magical.
And maybe, just maybe,
somewhere in the vastness of this cosmos...
we still do.

70 Beats per Minute

December 2024 Intimate Care

Been quite some time since we talked and I've been meaning to ask how you've been doing lately,
but I know how much you adore words,
so I'll try and say it your way.
I'm not great at this, but here's me… trying.

Hey lady!

It's been quite some time since I checked on you.
So, grab that pillow lying on the right side of your bed, behind your study table.
Pull it closer—close enough to feel its warmth on this chilly winter morning.
Think of it as me, hugging you, hands wrapped around your waist.

Now, take that hand of yours, the one that's been holding the pen all night,
and place it gently on your chest—like you're feeling the quiet rhythm
that reminds you you're alive.
Or like it's me, leaning in, resting my face on your chest,
ear pressed against the warm, bare skin
that holds the scars this world has left behind.
I can feel that warm, lively heart of yours.

Now tell me, what do I hear?
Is it steady?
Is it skipping beats like a nervous dancer?
Or is it right where it belongs—
at 70 beats per minute?

You Didn't Know, Now You Do

October 2024 Lingering Love & Letting Go

You ever have someone leave… but somehow still linger?
Yeah, that's her.

She's the one who taught me that rebel-in-a-white-vest kind of attitude.
Satisfy your soul, not the fcking society*—I learned that watching her live, not preach.
And now, she's gone.

But it's weird, right?
I still scroll back sometimes.
Not like I'm stuck, just… curious.
Like, is she okay? Did she smile today?
Did her favorite playlist still hit the same?

The truth? I saw someone else wearing my favorite shade of blue once.
That exact color I once associated with her.
And I just… acted like I never knew it. Like my memory didn't ache a little.

You feel something that real once, and even when it fades—you don't forget.
I don't chase. I don't beg. I don't text.
But sometimes… yeah, I still search.

And hey, if you're listening—
You didn't know back then, but now you know…
You did make me follow you.
Not in the lost-puppy kind of way.
Just enough to admit—yeah, I was in love.

But I'm good now.
Just had to say it. Not for you.
For me.

You, My Daily Prayer

September 2024 First Love & Letting Go

Now that it's all over, how am I even supposed to say it all directly?
These unsent words are all I have left—my only resort.
You do know how much you mean to me, right?
My first love, my lady, a soul so pure...
One among my everyday prayers,
A creature carved too perfectly to ever forget.

You know, I sometimes think—
maybe in some other universe, I didn't let go.
Maybe there, I held your hand tighter,
said all the things I stayed quiet about here.
Maybe in one of those scattered timelines,
you chose to stay.
And we… we walked home together every night.

But in this one, I'm just left behind,
learning how to swim alone in the dead sea of life.
I taught you enough, didn't I?
But what's the point if you can't swim without the life jacket I became for you?

And yet, why does it hurt this much—suddenly?
Maybe this is how we learn to let go.
Of people. Of things. Of quiet hopes.
Maybe one day, it won't sting as much.

After all, your happy face is all I wish to see—
Even if it's from far away,
Even if I'm no longer the reason for it.

No Way Back

August 2024 Lost Innocence & Survival

They keep sending men.
Colder, meaner, quieter.
Each one thinking he'll be the one to finish me.
But this? This ain't a duel.
It's just another Tuesday.

Same alley.
Same snow crunching beneath my boots.
Same breath fog in the air before the trigger gets pulled.
Face to face with death, again.

They look me in the eye and realize too late:
I've walked too far in this game to go back.
Normal life? That door closed years ago.

I don't shoot them.
I kill them.
The scissors come out first—
tongue, gone.
Then the eyes—
no more witnesses.

And when the hammer drops,
it's not rage.
It's rhythm.

Chest caves.
Skull cracks.
Snow turns red, slow and steady.

I don't talk much.
I let the blood do the explaining.
Then I dump the body in the valley.
Fresh snow hides everything by morning.

People say I'm a killer.
But all I've ever done is stay alive.

And tomorrow?
Death's coming back.
Same game.
Different face.

To the Departed

July 2025 Love & Loss

Since the day you left this physical form of yours,
I wonder what it's like to be one among those trillions
strolling in the cosmos.
I know you're always sticking around with me;
I wish there was some telepathy to talk.

You can hear me;
I wish I could hear you too.
I know I'll be able to one day,
facing you right above there,
without worrying about any constraints.

It's going to be a real chat;
I've got so much to tell.
Yet, I wish I could right now—
nature's been teaching me quite some patience.

Even after all this, I don't feel alone.
The air around me smells of you.
I walk around the streets,
to the places I'd been with you.
I see us right there,
sitting across the bar table
with your favorite wine.

I don't know why you're blinking at me from far away.
The sunlight that falls on my steam-ironed shirt
brings the warmth of your hug.
The chatter of birds in the trees
reminds me of your chirping.

The sunset across the horizon
brings back memories of you smiling,
wide and carefree.
When the world sleeps,
I feel like talking to you
in the stillness of night,
under this starry sky.

I see you as part of the nature around me;
I feel you within myself.

Before Sunrise

February 2024 Dawn Reflections & Distance

Every morning, at 4:30 a.m.,
I wake before the world stirs.
I reach for my phone,
still unlocking it with your birthday —
a habit I never unlearned.
I tell myself I don't check your texts anymore.
But sometimes, when sleep refuses to come, I do.

My room still carries your scent. My chair reminds me of the countless nights we spent, watching movies and sharing those 2 a.m. laughs.
Sometimes I catch you online.
Maybe you're studying, chasing your dreams as you always did.
Maybe you're singing softly to yourself.
Maybe you're just scrolling, numbing yourself to the world.
Or maybe, like me, you're just breathing in this delicate summer morning.

The moon drapes the city in silver.
The night recalls your black sweater, the trees your pearl bracelet, shimmering at first light.

The rustling of leaves carries your laughter across the breeze, that bright sound we spilled across rooftops while stargazing.
This morning, my mood matches the weather —
fragile, shifting, quietly aching.
I wish, foolishly, to ask how life feels on your side of the world.
But no — I won't.
No regrets.
Some things are meant to be admired from afar, not reclaimed.

I know you're doing fine.
And so am I, in ways that don't need explaining.

We both watch this new dawn rise,
silent witnesses from different worlds,
standing on opposite sides of the same mirror,
close enough to touch —
and yet, somehow, impossibly far away.

A Love That Stays

January 2025 Distance & Eternal Love

I wanted just one, only her,
Yet the universe chose distance instead.
Not a whisper, not a picture,
Just memories painted inside my head.

I love her in moments, in passing time,
In echoes only silence hears.
A selfish heart, unwilling to share,
Yet bound to longing through the years.

She is my calm, my quiet peace,
A fleeting dream I never chase.
For if she stays inside my mind,
She'll never leave, never erase.

Her eyes, the nebulas burning bright,

A force unseen, yet my infinity.

So let me love her from afar,

She'll be my final thought in death,
A story left, forever untold.

Loved, and Not by Me

March 2024 Unrequited Love & Letting Go

When did it start?
Maybe when I was imagining my entire lifetime with you—
from securing earnings to getting old, your hand in mine.
I spent nights thinking of anything but you.
You said things might not work.
I didn't realize the depth of those words—
those that trembled with fear.

With every passing day, my love for you grew even larger, like an eagle soaring high above the clouds,
transcending the boundaries I had set for my heart— to never let anyone touch it.

Remember how you once asked if you were able to enter my heart?
I didn't say much then.
I'd say you barged straight in, without taking off your shoes—
like a gush of fresh wind in a wooden cottage in the midst of a storm.

You do know how madly this little heart of mine was in love with you—
like a child's love for cotton candy,
like the moon's quiet longing for the ocean's tide.
And yet, in your mind, you chose him every time.
I did know what was to come,
yet I lied to this heart of mine.
My heart and mind existed in contradiction.

And yet, to come back to reality is
reminding myself that you're loved… and not by me.
Maybe in another universe, we're still together.
Maybe...
Isn't it just a hope I'm giving myself?
I shouldn't.

But what about this one?
What about your last words that echo in my mind...
the ones that tear apart my heart every day at 4:30 a.m.?
Not like I'm asking for any repayment—
but,
O Lord!
Am I being too selfish to ask her,
just for myself?

The Devil that Lurks

October 2024 Internal Darkness & Self-Confrontation

People think I've got anger issues, but I say I don't.
I assure myself that I've overcome it, but deep down, I fear the devil inside me.
I can feel it lurking deep within my core, waiting for a chance to strike.
Every time I think about it, I find myself wandering into an unknown territory in my mind.

It's a winter evening. The pines are covered with snow,
with snowflakes falling to the ground like tears in a melancholy setting.
I can see myself carrying a corpse of something unknown.
It has the same body complexion as mine, the same weight and height—
only, I'm unable to see its face.
Yet, I do not feel its weight on my shoulder; it all feels insubstantial.

At the same time, I hear the footsteps of someone walking beside me.
I can hear them breathing heavily, in and out, in this cold winter night,
and this is the moment fear strikes.
Not that I lack courage—I just wish not to look back.

This way, I make my way to the graveyard.
I put the corpse down, bring out my axe from the bag, and start digging.
When I'm finished, I'm astonished to find the grave just fits my body dimensions.
I pick up the thing, bury it deep inside, cover the surface, and clean myself up.

Now it's been a few thousand times that I've witnessed this moment again.
Tired and sick, I fall asleep.

When I wake up the next morning, I see it's all gone.
The grave has been dug up, and the corpse has disappeared.
I hear a cackle deep in the woods, just like the other day.
I pick up my gun and blood-laden axe and set out again to hunt it.

On my way, I ponder who did it.
But deep down, I'm afraid to look in the frozen lake.
I know I'll see my hands covered in snow and mud.

The Loop of You

July 2025 Cells Forget, But I Don't

1 A.M. — Night of July 17, her birthday.
It had been seven months since she went away.
Luca found himself once again sitting against the empty balcony of his room, scribbling thoughts into his notepad.

I read somewhere,
"I don't love you, but I'll love you forever."
Didn't really get what it meant then.
But maybe I do now.
Maybe it's about letting go.

But how do you just throw someone out of your life
and act as if they never existed?
How do you learn to let go of people you love?
We don't.
Of course—human tendencies, right?

Maybe we forget them with time...
Nah, not the case.
Considering how few people an average human ever truly grows close to,
and how short our lifespans are as a species,
it's rare we forget the ones we once dreamed of forever with.

What else remains, then?
...
So many cases, right?
Biologically, we become new people every 7–10 years.

Guess that's enough time to forget how your touch felt,
or the smell of your perfume,
or that wide, carefree smile of yours—
the one that reminded me of a rabbit.
The one that made even the horizon's glow fade away.

But there's a twist.
Even though almost every cell in our body changes,
the brain cells don't.
Now that's a tragedy.

Because even if I forget your voice,
or your phone number,
this mind of mine will still bring back those chapters
whenever I'm alone.

Maybe I'll stop overthinking someday.
Maybe I'll fall in love again—
harder this time.
Maybe I won't miss you.
Maybe losing you won't feel like regret anymore...

But as of now,
all I've got is the company of my keyboard,
my notepad,
and this brain that plays the day I met you
on a loop—back and forth.

Suddenly, a cool gust of wind brushed past his ears— utterly refreshing, calming his enraging soul.
It was about to rain.
He got up... and headed to sleep.

The Moon Girl

July 2025 Celestial Beauty & Enchantment

I met her the night
the city felt quieter than usual.
She stood there like a feather floating on a lake —
something so calm,
something you couldn't take your eyes off.

Her eyes — those that remind you of a star being born —
something so divine, you don't dare look too long.
Every inch of her body, like moonlight poured into human form.
Her voice, like the first raindrops falling on a lotus.

The sighs and pauses in her voice notes,
like the keys on my piano — each, perfectly placed.
Her very existence, like a mermaid who'd travelled across timelines just to see you.

She talked of the moon, like moon.
As if she'd been there once,
and left a part of herself behind.
Her talks, like a melodious note you wouldn't stop listening to,
like the kind of silence you don't want to break.

She didn't speak often — but when she did,
it felt like the moon leaned in too, just to listen.
Her words weren't loud.

They moved slow, like moonlight on water —
magical enough to make my heart pound,
and deep enough to make my soul realise its every breath.

I don't really know what it triggered inside me,
but that night, all I wanted was
to play my piano for her,
to write for her,
and to utterly, madly fall in love with her.
And to hope — just quietly —
that she'd stay long enough to hear the whole song.

One Sided - II
